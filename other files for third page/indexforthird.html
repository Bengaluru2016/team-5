Hasnabad Farmer Services Producer Company

Hasnabad Farmer Services Producer Company was set up in the year 2012 to collectivise small and marginal women farmers, located in Kodangal block of Mahabubnagar district, and to build a community owned professionally run company that would directly improve the livelihood and income of the member farmers. Kodangal block is well known for its gram cultivation, especially red gram. Hasnabad Producer Company has been promoted by our partner organisation, Access Livelihood Consulting, which is located in Hyderabad.

Little Drop Life Home

Little Drops Life Home is located in the Chandel district of Manipur, at a distance of about 80 kms from Imphal. It was started in the year 2010 with the mission of providing a safe haven to vulnerable children, who are either orphans or came from economically weak families. Chandel district lies in the hilly region of Manipur that is primarily populated by the indigenous tribal communities.

Meena Devi

The women of mountain region have the great potentiality to successfully carry out dairy farming. Meena Devi is one such woman hailing from Uttarakhand. She's is primarily involved in agriculture along with her husband upon which they earn about Rs 36000/- yearly. But it is a known fact that the prosperity of agriculture relies on varied factors like timely showers, fertilizers used etc. Anything unusual leaves the couple with little or nothing and they can hardly be able to have a three square meal with satisfaction. To make matters worse they will have to bear the extremities of weather as they live in a thatched roof. Further, they are deprived of a proper source of purifying the water. This has made her take up buffalo rearing where by she'll rear it and sell the milk, so as to add an extra rupee to their mere income. As such Meena wants to purchase a buffalo which costs Rs 45000/-.

Sarveshvari Devi

People living in the mountain side find cattle rearing favourable as there would be no problem for fodder. Sarveshwari Devi, hailing from Uttarakhand has planned to take up cow rearing. She and her husband work as a daily wage labourers and earn about Rs 4000/- a month. But this doesn't suffice to meet the needs of the family to the fullest. The educational expenses of her children studying 7th and 9th are soaring up with each passing year. Besides, there is no source for purifying the water. She wishes to increase her income by rearing cow and selling its milk. As such she requires a loan of Rs 35000/- to buy a cow. Please help Sarveshwari Devi and enable her to raise her living and thereby to fulfill the basic needs.

Seeta Devi

Seeta Devi aged 31 of Rudraprayag district in Uttarakhand wants to set up a dairy farming business for which she has applied for a loan of Rs 35000. It requires 30K for milking buffalo and cattle feed of Rs 5000. Dairy farming is the most potential activities for the poor women of the mountain region, however, it remains at subsistence level. It will generate enough amount of income to pay all the necessary expenses. She will be able to have a better life and support her family with proper education for children.
